B .J . Habibie

One of the Greatest Indonesian President Ever.

You must recognize BJ Habibie as a major Indonesian figure. Has a full name Prof. DR (HC). Ing. Dr. Sc. Mult. Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie, FREng is the 3rd President of the Republic of Indonesia. The man who was born in Pare-pare is the 4th child of eight children. Married to Hasri Ainun Habibie on May 12, 1962, he has two children, namely Ilham Akbar and Thareq Kemal. You can also see the struggles during his life from the films Habibie & Ainun, Rudy Habibie, to Habibie & Ainun 3.

Habibie's childhood was spent with his siblings in Pare-Pare, South Sulawesi. Habibie has shown a firm nature in adhering to principles since childhood. Habibie, who has a passion for horse riding, had to lose his father, who died on September 3, 1950, due to a heart attack. Shortly after his father died, Habibie moved to Bandung to study at Governments Middlebare School. In high school, he began to show his outstanding achievements, especially in exact subjects. Habibie is a favorite figure in his school.

After graduating from high school in Bandung in 1954, he entered the University of Indonesia in Bandung (now ITB). He received his Diploma from Technische Hochschule, Germany in 1960 which then received a Doctorate degree from the same place in 1965. Habibie married in 1962 and has two children. In 1967, he became an honorary professor (Professor) at the Bandung Institute of Technology.

Habibie's steps were admired by many, full of controversy, many admirers but not a few who disagreed with him. Every time, the winner of the prestigious Theodore van Karman Award, returns from his German habitat, he is always in the news. Habibie only studied at ITB Bandung for a year, 10 years studying until he earned a doctorate in aircraft construction in Germany with the title Summa Cum laude. Then he worked in the leading aircraft industry, MBB Gmbh Germany, before fulfilling President Soeharto's call to return to Indonesia.

In Indonesia, Habibie for 20 years served as Minister of State for Research and Technology / Head of BPPT, led 10 state-owned strategic industry companies, was elected by the MPR to become Vice President of the Republic of Indonesia, and was sworn in by the Chairman of the Supreme Court to become President of the Republic of Indonesia to replace Soeharto. Soeharto handed over the presidency to Habibie based on Article 8 of the 1945 Constitution. Until finally Habibie was also forced to resign due to the East Timor referendum which chose independence. His accountability speech was rejected by the MPR RI. He also returned to being an ordinary citizen and again moved to Germany.
